Counterpoint Player

Randomly generated polyphonic music following the rules of species counterpoint, played and analysed in real time.

Open the Player

Rule-based generation

2–4 voice counterpoint with consonant intervals, no parallel fifths or octaves, contrary motion, stepwise melodies, leap recovery, and proper cadences.

6 instruments

Piano, Harpsichord, Church Organ, Baroque Organ, Reed Organ, and Flute Organ — all synthesized live with the Web Audio API.

Score notation

Canvas-rendered staves with clefs, key signatures, time signatures, bar lines, diatonic note placement, accidentals, ledger lines, ties, and a moving playhead.

Live analysis

Scale degrees, interval quality, cadence detection (PAC, IAC, HC, plagal, deceptive), voice motion types, and counterpoint rule-checking updated on every beat.

12 keys

Major and minor keys with correct key signatures and harmonic-minor raised leading tones at cadences.

Continuous mode

Check one box and the music plays indefinitely, generating new pieces seamlessly at the end of each one.

How it works

The bass voice is generated first as a cantus firmus with variable note durations. Upper voices are layered on top, each note scored against harmonic and melodic constraints and the best candidate chosen. The result is played through additive/subtractive synthesizers built entirely from Web Audio oscillators, filters, and envelopes. No samples, no libraries — pure browser-native synthesis.
